Frequently Asked Questions

The Remote Experimental Team at Rosemary Hill Observatory (RETRHO) is a University of Florida organization dedicated to observing, recording, and publishing data on space phenomena. Under Dr. Jamie Tayar's guidance, the RETRHO team conducts regular observations, develops data analysis tools, and collaborates on research projects to better understand celestial events. The team provides hands-on experience for students interested in astronomy, astrophysics, and data science while contributing valuable observations to the scientific community.
Any student with an interest in astronomy, data analysis, or space exploration is welcome to join! No prior experience is necessary—just enthusiasm! For more information, reach out to Dr. Tayar.

To sign up for an observing session, follow these steps:

  1. Fill out the Sign-Up Form.
  2. Check the schedule spreadsheet to see how many people are signed up for each day.
  3. If you need to correct your submission, email uf.retrho@gmail.com so that someone in pre-obs can follow the correction procedures.

Observing Guidelines & Reminders:

  • A maximum of five junior observers can sign up per session.
  • At least one senior observer must be present.
  • GO/NO GO will be announced by the senior observer on the day of observing.
  • Plan to create an observing plan about an hour before sunset.
  • Approximate sunset times can be found when creating an event in the RETRHO Google Calendar.
  • If you will be late or absent, notify your observing team and senior observer.
