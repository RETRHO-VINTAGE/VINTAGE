Telescope at Rosemary Hill Observatory

Telescope and Camera Specifications

The Rosemary Hill Observatory houses a 14-inch Meade telescope, which replaced an older 18-inch telescope to improve automation and ease of use. It's equipped with an SBIG STF-8300 camera, designed to capture high-quality images of the night sky. This camera, combined with a variety of broadband and narrowband filters, allows astronomers to study different wavelengths of light and helps them observe everything from bright stars to faint nebulae. Thanks to funding from the University of Florida, the observatory has been upgraded for remote operation from campus. These upgrades include automated dome controls, real-time telescope positioning using advanced image calibration, weather monitoring systems for safe operation, and quick processing of images as they're captured.

The telescope is paired with an SBIG STF-8300M camera, which is designed for capturing high-resolution images of celestial objects. The camera has a 5.4 µm pixel size and a sensor size of 18.0 mm by 13.5 mm, allowing it to capture detailed images across a wide field of view. Its images have a resolution of 3326 x 2504 pixels, and it supports 3x3 binning, which can enhance sensitivity when observing faint objects. Together with the telescope's focal length and filters, this camera setup provides a balanced combination of detail and sensitivity, making it well-suited for both bright stars and deep-sky objects like nebulae and galaxies.
